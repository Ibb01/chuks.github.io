Welcome

I am glad you made it to my page today, and for that reason I say DAALU!!!

My journey as a Data Analyst began in 2015 as an Undergraduate researcher in Physics. During my time in Physics, I worked to develop a prototype for a Bladeless Wind turbine. This process required 3 years of collecting simulation results measuring wind speed, airflow/wind direction, and pressure differential. After the data collection was completed and compiled, I analyzed which of these three combinations gave the best outcome, using Excel. My research was a success, as I was able to present my findings for my Capstone which was a main requirement for my degree.

Another key experience was when I served as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Field of Health and Human Performance. The goal of my research was to find a correlation, if any, between compensatory movement patterns and possible injury occurrence amongst the school’s athletes. In this role, I had to first figure out which compensatory movement patterns to look for in athletes based on the sports they play. Asking this initial question helped pinpoint what data to look for and collect. It was equally in this role that I learned how to use SQL due to the nature of the experiment I was conducting.

Most recently, I have been busy finetuning my analytic skill set by becoming certified/licensed in SQL, D3.js, AWS, and Oracle Database, as you saw in my Portfolio. I am also currently enrolled in Google’s Data Analytics Certification Course. So, if you made it this far, then I believe we are in agreement that you should reach out to me because you know that I will love to support your vision and growth with my experience in Research and Data Analysis. Thank you for your time and consideration.

I look forward to hearing from you soon!
